Research

My research interests lie mostly in extremal graph theory, with an emphasis on probabilistic methods. A lot of my research has been in the area of word-representable graphs, though recently I have branched out to Ramsey numbers and symmetries of graphs as well.

Publications

  • Improving $R(3,k)$ in just two bites, with P. Horn, D. King, and F. Pfender; preprint. URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.19718
  • On Edge-Resilience and Subdivision-Resilience of Non-Word-Representable Graphs; with E. Dombi, H. Hameed, P. Horn, S. Kitaev, and A. Pyatkin; submitted.
  • Word-representation numbers of graphs: Bottlenecks and bounds, with P. Horn, C. Muir, and A. Owens; submitted.
  • k-11-representations of graphs, revisited, with P. Horn and C. Muir; Proceedings of the Edinburgh Mathematical Society (2025), 1-15.
  • Word-Representable Graphs: Orientations, Posets, and Bounds, with P. Horn, C. Muir, and A. Owens; Electron. J. Combin. 31 (2024), no. 4, Paper No. 4.2, 26 pp.
  • The Frobenius problem over number fields with a real embedding, with A. Feiner; Semigroup Forum 108 (2024), no. 1, 73–100.
